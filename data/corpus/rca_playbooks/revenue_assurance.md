# Revenue Assurance in Telecom — Best Practices

## What is Revenue Assurance?
Revenue Assurance (RA) is the practice of ensuring that all services delivered by a telecom operator are accurately captured, rated, billed, and collected. The TM Forum estimates that telecom operators lose 1-5% of revenue annually due to billing leakage.

## Revenue Leakage Sources

### Network-to-Billing Leakage
- CDRs generated by network but lost before reaching billing system
- Typical loss point: mediation device buffer overflow
- Detection: Compare network CDR count with billing CDR count

### Rating Leakage
- CDRs processed but rated at incorrect (lower) amounts
- Typical cause: tariff configuration errors, promotional override bugs
- Detection: Sample audit of rated vs. expected amounts

### Billing Leakage
- Rated CDRs not appearing on customer bills
- Typical cause: billing cycle boundary errors, account status issues
- Detection: Reconcile rated revenue with billed revenue

### Collection Leakage
- Billed amounts not collected from customers
- Typical cause: payment processing failures, incorrect dunning
- Detection: Aging analysis of accounts receivable

## Key Revenue Assurance Controls

### 1. CDR Reconciliation
- End-to-end CDR count reconciliation (network → mediation → billing)
- Threshold: ≤0.1% variance is acceptable
- Frequency: Daily automated reconciliation
- Alert if variance exceeds threshold

### 2. Rating Audit
- Sample 1% of rated CDRs and manually verify charges
- Compare with tariff plan documentation
- Focus on: new plans, recently modified plans, promotional rates
- Frequency: Weekly

### 3. Billing Completeness
- Verify all active accounts receive bills on schedule
- Check for zero-value bills on accounts with known usage
- Verify tax and regulatory fee calculations
- Frequency: Per billing cycle

### 4. Revenue Trending
- Track revenue metrics over time (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Flag significant deviations (>5% week-over-week)
- Segment by: plan type, service type, customer segment, geography
- Statistical methods: z-score, exponential smoothing, control charts

## Anomaly Detection for Revenue Assurance

### Statistical Methods
- **Z-score threshold:** Flag transactions >3σ from mean
- **Grubbs' test:** Identify single outliers in normally distributed data
- **IQR method:** Flag values below Q1-1.5×IQR or above Q3+1.5×IQR

### Machine Learning Methods
- **Isolation Forest:** Efficient for high-dimensional anomaly detection; works well with mixed feature types
- **DBSCAN:** Density-based clustering; identifies anomalies as noise points
- **Autoencoders:** Neural network approach; learns normal billing patterns and flags deviations
- **One-Class SVM:** Learns boundary of normal data; useful for highly imbalanced datasets

### Ensemble Approach
Best practice: Combine multiple detection methods:
1. Rule-based checks (known patterns, business rules)
2. Statistical methods (distribution-based outlier detection)
3. ML models (pattern-based anomaly detection)
4. Flag as anomaly if ≥2 methods agree (consensus approach)
